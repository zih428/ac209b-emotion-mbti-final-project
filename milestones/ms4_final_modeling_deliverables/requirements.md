## Final Report

The report should communicate final modeling work and findings to a reader who has not seen prior milestones.

Required elements:

1. Title, group Canvas number, and group member names.
2. Background and motivation.
3. Problem statement.
4. Data and EDA.
5. Methods and models.
6. Results and interpretation.
7. Conclusions and discussion.
8. Broader impact.
9. References.

Do not re-paste the full MS2/MS3 EDA. Distill the findings that materially influenced final modeling decisions.

## Video Presentation

- Hard time limit: 6 minutes.
- Must play at 1.0x normal speed.
- All team members must appear on camera and speak unless accommodated.
- Submit as an unlisted YouTube link or Google Drive link.
- Link must be viewable without sign-in and remain accessible until June 1, 2026.

Suggested video arc:

1. Introductions.
2. Background, motivation, and problem statement.
3. Data and key EDA findings.
4. Modeling approach and training details.
5. Results and interpretation.
6. Conclusions and future work.

## Code Notebook

- Name the main notebook clearly, for example `cs1090b_ms4_main_group66.ipynb`.
- Main notebook should be readable to someone who has not seen the project.
- Use clear headings, prose explanations, and inline comments/docstrings.
- Include group Canvas number and member names.
- Document dependencies and runtime configuration.
- "Restart kernel + Run All" should succeed with documented dependencies and data access.
- Main notebook can import helper `.py` modules or supporting notebooks.

## General Expectations

- Quality and thoroughness of thinking over visual polish.
- Cite all outside resources, datasets, code, and generative AI assistance.
- Report results honestly and compare to baselines.
- Keep visuals readable and clean.
